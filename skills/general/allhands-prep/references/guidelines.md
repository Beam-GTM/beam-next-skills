# Beam All-Hands Guidelines

> How we run all-hands meetings at Beam

---

## Purpose

All-hands meetings exist to:
- Align the entire company around what matters most right now
- Create energy and momentum
- Connect teams to customers and to each other
- Reinforce our values through real examples

They are **not** for:
- Status updates that could be async
- Debating decisions already made
- Airing grievances or problems without solutions

---

## Format

### Duration
**25-35 minutes max.** Respect people's time. If it can't be said in 35 minutes, it's too much for one session.

### Standard Structure

| Section | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Opening | 2 mins | Set the energy and theme |
| Team Updates | 5-7 mins | Quick wins and what's coming |
| Main Topic | 10-15 mins | The core message of this all-hands |
| The Ask | 3 mins | What we need from everyone |
| Close | 2 mins | Land the message, create momentum |

---

## Team Updates

### Format
Each department lead gets **60-90 seconds**. No exceptions.

### What to cover
- **One thing shipped** — what did we deliver since last all-hands?
- **One thing coming** — what's next?
- **One number** (optional) — if there's a metric worth sharing

### What to avoid
- Long explanations or context-setting
- Lists of everything the team is working on
- Problems without solutions
- Anything that requires slides

> 💡 **Prep requirement:** Leads should know exactly what they're going to say before the all-hands. No winging it.

---

## Main Topic

Each all-hands should have **one clear theme**. Not three things. One.

### Good themes
- Customer obsession (with real examples)
- A specific value we need to reinforce
- A strategic shift everyone needs to understand
- Celebrating a major win and what we learned

### How to structure it
1. **Frame the why** — Why does this matter right now?
2. **Make it concrete** — Real examples, real people, real outcomes
3. **Show the team effort** — How different functions contributed
4. **Connect to values** — Tie it back to who we are

### Featuring team members
When someone other than the lead presents:
- Give them a **specific, narrow scope** (2 mins max)
- Prep them on exactly what to cover
- Keep it authentic — messy and real beats polished and generic

---

## Tone & Delivery

### Energy level: 8/10
Fired up, but not manic. Confident, not desperate.

### Pace: Brisk
Don't linger. Forward motion in the delivery mirrors forward motion in the message.

### Be specific
No vague "let's do our best" — actual examples, actual names, actual expectations.

### What to avoid

| ❌ Don't say | ✅ Say instead |
|-------------|----------------|
| "We're going to try to..." | "We're doing..." |
| "I hope everyone can..." | "Here's what I need from you..." |
| "Does this sound good?" | "This is the direction. Questions?" |
| Long explanations of problems | Brief context, then focus on solutions |

---

## Slides

### Less is more
- **Max 8-10 slides** for a 30-minute all-hands
- One idea per slide
- Visuals over walls of text

### Standard slide types
1. **Title** — Theme + Beam logo
2. **Mission/values connection** — Why this matters
3. **Framework or model** (if applicable) — One visual that captures the concept
4. **Team effort visual** — How functions connect
5. **Customer examples** — 1 slide per example, keep it tight
6. **The Ask** — What we need from each team
7. **Close** — One memorable line

## Prep Checklist

### 1 week before
- [ ] Confirm theme and main speaker
- [ ] Identify any team members who will present
- [ ] Start slide deck

### 3 days before
- [ ] Slides complete
- [ ] Notify department leads of update format
- [ ] Prep any featured speakers on their segment

### 1 day before
- [ ] Run-through with any speakers
- [ ] Final slide review
- [ ] Send calendar reminder with agenda

### Day of
- [ ] Tech check 10 mins before
- [ ] Start on time, end on time

---

## After the All-Hands

### Immediately after
- Post slides or key takeaways in Slack
- If there were specific asks, reiterate them in writing

### Follow-through
- If you committed to something, do it
- Reference the all-hands theme in team meetings that week
- Make it real, not just a moment

---

## Principles

1. **Respect time.** If we're pulling everyone together, it needs to be worth it.

2. **One message.** People remember one thing. Make sure you know what that one thing is.

3. **Show, don't tell.** Real examples > abstract concepts. Name names. Share specifics.

4. **Energy is contagious.** The tone you bring is the tone the team will carry out.

5. **Forward motion only.** All-hands should leave people energized to act, not drained from listening.

---

## Planning Considerations

### Multiple all-hands spacing
If two all-hands are close together (e.g., one this week, one before Christmas):
- Make them distinct (one working session, one lighter/celebratory)
- Expect lower attendance for holiday-adjacent meetings
- Adjust expectations accordingly

### Deck ownership & prep timing
- **Deck owner assigned** (not assumed)
- **Mid-week check-in** on deck status
- **Don't wait until day-of** to review

### Leadership/Wisdom sessions are separate
- **All-hands = whole company, one theme, 30 mins**
- **Leadership/Wisdom sessions = different format, different purpose**
