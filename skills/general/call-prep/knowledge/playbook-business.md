# Buyer Persona: Business Function Buyer

**Who:** Head of Operations, Head of Finance, VP HR, Head of CX, Shared Services Lead
**Their world:** They own a team doing manual, repetitive work. They have budget pressure, headcount constraints, and compliance requirements. They may or may not know what AI agents are — they just want their problem solved.

---

## The 7-Beat Arc (Business Function Variant)

### Beat 1: YOUR WORLD

**What to research:**
- How many people are in their function (LinkedIn headcount by department)
- What systems they use (job postings mention tools, integrations page on website)
- Regulatory environment (industry-specific compliance: SOX, GDPR, PCI, etc.)
- Growth signals (hiring for ops roles = scaling manually; not hiring = cost pressure)

**What to say:**
> "Before we talk about Beam, let me share what we've learned about [company]. You're running [function] with approximately [X] people across [Y] locations. Your team handles [specific volume] of [specific workflow] — mostly manually, using [systems]. You're dealing with [specific pressure: growing volume, compliance requirements, error rates, headcount freeze]."

> "Tell us where we're wrong."

**Duration:** 1 minute talking, then 5-10 minutes listening.

---

### Beat 2: YOUR FRICTION

**Default friction pattern for business function buyers:**

| Friction | How to spot it | What to say |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Volume exceeding capacity** | They're hiring ops roles, or complaining about backlog | "Your volume is growing faster than your team. Every new hire is a linear cost increase for a problem that should scale differently." |
| **Error rates with real consequences** | Regulated industry, or they mention rework/corrections | "Manual processes have a 2-5% error rate. At your volume, that's [X] errors per month — each one costing time, money, or compliance risk." |
| **Tribal knowledge risk** | Key person dependency, or they mention knowledge transfer | "Your processes work because specific people know the exceptions. If those people leave, you have a business continuity problem." |
| **Failed automation attempts** | They mention RPA, macros, or "we tried" | "You've invested in automation before. It worked for the simple stuff but broke on exceptions. The 80% that's easy wasn't the problem — the 20% that's hard is where the cost lives." |

**Then ask:** "Which of these resonates most? And what are we missing?"

---

### Beat 3: THE FAILED PATHS

> "Most companies we talk to have already tried to solve this. Three patterns we see:"

| Path | What happened | Why it didn't work |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| **RPA / workflow automation** | "It works for the happy path but breaks on exceptions" | Rules-based systems can't handle judgment, edge cases, or unstructured input |
| **Internal AI project** | "The team spent 6-12 months and it still isn't in production" | Building reliable agents requires infrastructure (eval, monitoring, improvement loops) that takes years to develop |
| **Consultancy** | "They designed a solution, left a report, and nothing was built" | Strategy without execution. The hard part isn't knowing what to build — it's building agents that actually work at 95%+ accuracy |

> "We've been through all of these ourselves. That's why we exist — to do the part that's hardest."

---

### Beat 4: A BETTER FUTURE

> "Imagine your [function] team in 6 months. The [specific workflow] that today takes [X hours/week] runs itself — 24/7, with 95%+ accuracy. Your team spends their time on judgment, escalations, and the work that actually needs a human. You're not just faster — you're operating at a fundamentally different level."

**Make it concrete to their world:**

| If they're in... | Paint this picture |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Operations | "Your shared inbox processes itself. Orders enter the ERP automatically. Exceptions are flagged, not lost." |
| Finance | "Invoices reconcile themselves. Collections happen on cadence without anyone chasing. Your month-end close takes days, not weeks." |
| HR | "CVs are screened in seconds, not hours. Your recruiters spend time on candidates who matter, not filtering noise." |
| CX | "Tickets are classified, drafted, and resolved without a human touching 60%+ of them. Your team handles the hard stuff." |

---

### Beat 5: WHY US

**For business function buyers, frame through outcomes, not technology:**

> "We've deployed this exact type of agent before — in your industry, for this workflow. Here's what happened."

**Pick 2 proof points from the vertical cheat sheet. Tell them as stories, not stats.**

Example (AR Collections):
> "BID-Coburg is a debt collection firm handling thousands of cases. Their team was spending all day extracting data from debtor correspondence and classifying responses. We deployed an agent that does this with 94% accuracy. They're projecting €5.2 million in annual savings. Not from cutting people — from redirecting them to cases that need human judgment."

**Then:** the self-learning story (60-second version from master playbook).

**Then:** accountability.
> "We don't hand over and leave. We own the outcome. If the agent underperforms, that's our problem."

---

### Beat 6: YOUR FIRST AGENT

> "Based on what you've told us, here's where we'd start."

Present 3 beachhead options from the call prep checklist. For each:

```
[Workflow name]
What it does: [1-2 sentences in their language, not ours]
Why this one: [Ties to friction they confirmed in Beat 2]
Expected impact: [FTE hours saved, error reduction, speed — use proof point analogy]
Package: [Map to repeatable package from catalog]
```

**Recommend one.** Don't leave it open.

> "We'd recommend Option A. Here's why: it has the highest volume, the clearest ROI, and we've done this exact build [X] times before. We can be in production in 6-8 weeks."

---

### Beat 7: THE PATH FORWARD

> "Three steps."

```
Step 1: DISCOVERY (1-2 weeks)
  We go deep on the workflow. Your team gives us 2-3 hours.
  We map the process, the exceptions, the data, the systems.
  We build the evaluation dataset.

Step 2: BUILD & DEPLOY (4-6 weeks)
  We build against your real data.
  You see it working before it goes live.
  95%+ accuracy validated before production.

Step 3: OPERATE & EXPAND
  Agent goes live. We monitor, optimize, own the outcome.
  Then we talk about agent #2.
```

**Bold close:**
> "We'd like to do option 1 — we build it together. Can we book a 30-minute discovery session with your [specific team] next week?"

## Common Mistakes with Business Function Buyers

| Mistake | Why it happens | What to do instead |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Talking about "agents" and "AI" too much | We're excited about tech | Use their language: "automated process," "digital worker," "the system handles it" |
| Showing the platform too early | They ask "can I see it?" | Redirect: "Absolutely — but first, let me make sure what I show you is relevant to your situation." |
| Proposing too many options | We want to show breadth | Recommend ONE beachhead. Have 2 alternatives if they push back. |
| Skipping the failed paths beat | Feels negative | It's the most credibility-building beat. It shows you understand their world, not just your product. |
| Underselling the expansion | Fear of overselling | Plant the seed: "The first agent proves it works. The real value is agents #2, #3, #4 — your operational AI layer." |
