# HR & Recruitment — Sales Cheat Sheet

**Grab this 10 minutes before any HR / recruitment call.**

---

## Signals to Research (5 min)

| Signal | Where to look | What it means |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| "We're hiring X00 roles this quarter" | LinkedIn jobs page, press releases | High-volume screening pain — direct beachhead fit |
| Multiple recruiter job postings | Their own careers page | Team is overwhelmed, can't keep up with inbound volume |
| Using staffing agencies | LinkedIn, vendor announcements | Already paying premium for capacity — budget exists |
| ATS mentioned in job descriptions | Job posts ("experience with Greenhouse/Workday") | Tells you their stack and integration surface |
| High Glassdoor complaints about hiring speed | Glassdoor reviews from candidates | Candidate experience is suffering — urgency driver |

---

## Default Friction Points

1. **Screening bottleneck at top of funnel** — Recruiters spend 60-70% of time on unqualified candidates, leaving no time for high-value sourcing or candidate experience.
2. **Candidate drop-off from slow response** — When screening takes days, top candidates accept other offers; time-to-fill balloons.
3. **Inconsistent evaluation criteria** — Different recruiters score differently, creating bias risk and compliance exposure.
4. **Manual scheduling coordination** — Back-and-forth emails for interview scheduling waste hours per candidate and frustrate both sides.

## What They've Probably Tried

- **Resume parsing tools** (Textkernel, Sovren) — extract data but don't evaluate fit; still need a human to review every parsed resume.
- **ATS built-in scoring** — keyword matching only, no nuanced evaluation; high false-positive rate floods recruiters with bad matches.
- **Outsourced screening to RPO/agencies** — expensive, inconsistent quality, and creates dependency without building internal capability.

---

## Common Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We need the human touch in hiring" | The agent handles the 80% of obvious yes/no decisions. Your recruiters spend their human touch on the 20% that actually need judgment — finalist interviews, culture fit, selling the role. |
| "Our roles are too specialized for AI to screen" | Booth runs 18,951 screenings across diverse roles at 97.78% completion. The agent learns your specific criteria — it's not keyword matching, it's evaluating against your rubric. |
| "We already have an ATS with built-in screening" | ATS scoring is keyword matching. Our agent reads context — career progression, transferable skills, red flags. That's why Booth saw 70% faster screening with better quality. |
| "What about bias and compliance?" | The agent applies your criteria consistently to every candidate — no Monday-morning vs. Friday-afternoon variance. Every decision is logged and auditable. More consistent than human screening. |
