# Competitive Positioning (Internal Only)

When prospects mention competitors, here's how to reframe:

| They mention | What they're really saying | Your response |
|-------------|--------------------------|---------------|
| "We're evaluating N8N / Make / Zapier" | "We want to build it ourselves cheaply" | "Those are great for simple workflows. When you need 95%+ accuracy on complex processes with exceptions, edge cases, and compliance — that's where self-learning agents differ from workflow automation." |
| "We have an internal AI team" | "We might build it ourselves" | "Perfect — we work alongside internal teams, not instead of them. The question is: do you want them spending 9-12 months on agent infrastructure, or deploying production agents in 6-8 weeks while they focus on differentiated work?" |
| "McKinsey / Accenture proposed an AI strategy" | "A consultancy promised us transformation" | "Strategy is valuable. But we've seen the pattern: strategy phase takes 6 months, then the implementation partner spends another 12 months and delivers 30-40% accuracy. We go from zero to production in 6-8 weeks. The strategy emerges from doing, not from slides." |
| "We're looking at ServiceNow / UiPath / Automation Anywhere" | "We think this is an RPA problem" | "RPA works for deterministic, rule-based tasks. The moment you have exceptions, judgment calls, or unstructured input — RPA breaks. That's exactly where AI agents excel, and that's where the real FTE cost is." |
| "We're using Claude / GPT directly via API" | "We've already started building" | "Smart. You've proven the technology works. The question now is: how do you govern it, audit it, improve it continuously, and scale it beyond the engineering team? That's the gap between a prototype and a production agent." |
