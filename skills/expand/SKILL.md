# /expand

Turn a bullet point or brief note into a fully developed paragraph.

## Usage

User provides:
- **Content to expand** — bullet point, note, or short text
- **Context** (optional) — what it's for (email, blog post, presentation)
- **Tone** (optional) — formal, casual, persuasive, etc.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Core Idea

Identify:
- What's the main point?
- What's implied but not stated?
- What supporting details would strengthen it?

### Step 2: Expand with Structure

Transform the bullet into a paragraph that:
- Opens with the key insight (not "This is about...")
- Adds 1-2 supporting details or examples
- Connects to broader implications if relevant
- Maintains the original voice/intent

### Step 3: Present Options

Offer 2 versions:
1. **Concise** (~2-3 sentences) — punchy, direct
2. **Full** (~4-5 sentences) — more developed, with context

## Examples

**Input**: "AI tools are changing how we work"

**Concise**:
> AI tools are fundamentally reshaping knowledge work. Tasks that once took hours—research, drafting, analysis—now happen in minutes. The winners won't be those who resist, but those who learn to direct these tools effectively.

**Full**:
> AI tools are fundamentally reshaping how knowledge workers operate. What used to take hours—deep research, first drafts, data analysis—now happens in minutes with the right prompts. This isn't about replacement; it's about amplification. The professionals who thrive won't be the ones who resist these tools, but those who learn to direct them like skilled conductors leading an orchestra. The question isn't whether to adopt AI, but how quickly you can integrate it into your workflow.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- ❌ Starting with "This means..." or "The point is..."
- ❌ Adding fluff without substance
- ❌ Losing the original voice or intent
- ❌ Over-explaining obvious implications
