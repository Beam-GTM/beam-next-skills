# Lead Research & Fit Assessment

Research prospects and assess whether they fit Beam's focus on automating outsourced work (BPO/RPO, document processing, data extraction at scale).

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context

**Collect all available information:**
- Email thread / initial outreach
- Company website
- LinkedIn profiles of attendees
- Any prior relationship history
- Meeting context (who initiated, why now)

**Web search the company:**
- What do they do? (business model)
- Who are their customers?
- What products/services do they offer?
- Recent news, funding, leadership changes

### Step 2: Understand Their Problem

**Parse the initial communication for signals:**
- What problem are they describing?
- What language are they using? ("manual", "repetitive", "scale", "automate")
- Who reached out? (decision-maker, influencer, researcher)
- What's the urgency? (new mandate, pain point, exploratory)

**Research their likely pain points:**
- What work in their business is labor-intensive?
- What processes are document-heavy?
- Where might they use outsourcing or BPOs today?

### Step 3: Assess Fit with Beam

**Load fit criteria:** See [references/beam-fit-criteria.md](references/beam-fit-criteria.md)

**Score against key criteria:**

| Criteria | Assessment | Notes |
|----------|------------|-------|
| High volume work? | ✅/⚠️/❌ | |
| Repetitive/structured? | ✅/⚠️/❌ | |
| Clear inputs/outputs? | ✅/⚠️/❌ | |
| Manual labor today? | ✅/⚠️/❌ | |
| Decision-maker access? | ✅/⚠️/❌ | |
| Budget signals? | ✅/⚠️/❌ | |

**Determine verdict:**
- ✅ **Strong fit** — Pursue actively
- ⚠️ **Potential fit** — Worth exploring, need more discovery
- ❌ **Poor fit** — Politely decline or deprioritize

### Step 4: Generate Call Prep Document

**Create a dated file:** `{YYYY-MM-DD}-{company}-call-prep.md`

**Include sections:**
1. **Key Participants** — Who's on the call, their roles
2. **Context** — Summary of the relationship/outreach
3. **About the Company** — What they do, their market
4. **Their Problem** — What we understand about their pain
5. **Fit Assessment** — Score against Beam criteria
6. **Discovery Questions** — Prioritized questions to ask
7. **What to Share About Beam** — Relevant proof points
8. **Potential Use Cases** — Hypothetical applications (don't pitch, discover)
9. **Call Flow** — Suggested timing and structure
10. **Red/Green Flags** — What signals to watch for
11. **Checklist** — Things to confirm before the call ends

### Step 5: Prepare Discovery Questions

**Discovery-first approach:**
- Lead with understanding, not pitching
- Ask about the problem before proposing solutions
- Quantify the pain (volume, cost, time, quality)
- Understand decision process

**Template questions:**
- "Can you walk me through what [their process] looks like day-to-day?"
- "Where does the source data come from?"
- "How many people work on this today?"
- "What's the biggest bottleneck — speed, cost, quality, or finding people?"
- "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
- "Who else would need to be involved if we found something promising?"
