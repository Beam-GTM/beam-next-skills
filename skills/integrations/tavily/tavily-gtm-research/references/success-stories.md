## Example Success Stories

### Closed Enterprise Deal with Market Intelligence

**Role:** Vertical Lead - Finance
**Date:** 2026-01-16
**Time Saved:** 3 hours → 15 minutes (92% reduction)

**Challenge:**
Needed comprehensive market intelligence for finance automation to support enterprise deal. Traditional research would take 3+ hours across multiple sources.

**Approach:**
```
1. Search Tavily for "workflow automation trends finance teams 2026"
2. Search Tavily for "finance automation ROI statistics" only from gartner.com and forrester.com
3. Search Tavily for "finance automation case studies 2026"
```

**Result:**
- Found 54.2% of finance teams stuck in partial automation (key stat)
- Identified 33.3% pursuing hyperautomation (market opportunity)
- Discovered shift to strategic work trend (value prop)
- Used insights in proposal, deal closed 2 weeks later

**Key Learnings:**
- Advanced search depth provides more comprehensive results
- Domain filtering to analyst firms gives credible statistics
- Real-time data (2026) more impactful than training data

## Lessons Learned

### What to Do

✅ **Be Specific in Queries**
- Vague queries = poor results
- Specific queries = targeted insights

✅ **Use Domain Filtering**
- Saves time sorting results
- Improves credibility
- Consistent quality

✅ **Add Time Context**
- "2026" ensures current data
- Quarterly specificity when relevant
- Avoids outdated information

✅ **Document Your Process**
- Share what works
- Help team learn faster
- Build institutional knowledge

### What to Avoid

❌ **Don't Skip Domain Filtering**
- Wastes time on low-quality sources
- Reduces credibility
- Inconsistent results

❌ **Don't Use Vague Queries**
- "AI" vs. "AI automation in finance 2026"
- Specificity matters

❌ **Don't Ignore Time Context**
- Outdated data undermines insights
- Always add year when relevant

❌ **Don't Research in Isolation**
- Share findings with team
- Build on each other's work
- Avoid duplicate research
