# /compress

Reduce content length by ~30% while keeping the core message intact.

## Usage

User provides:
- **Content to compress** — paragraph, section, or full document
- **Target reduction** (optional) — specific percentage or word count

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify What to Cut

Look for:
- **Redundant phrases**: "in order to" → "to"
- **Filler words**: "really", "very", "basically", "actually"
- **Throat-clearing**: "I think that", "It's worth noting that"
- **Duplicate ideas**: Same point said twice differently
- **Weak qualifiers**: "somewhat", "kind of", "a bit"

### Step 2: Tighten Structure

- Combine sentences that cover related points
- Remove unnecessary transitions ("Furthermore", "Additionally")
- Cut examples if the point is already clear
- Eliminate meta-commentary ("As mentioned above")

### Step 3: Present Result

Show:
- Original word count
- New word count
- Percentage reduced
- The compressed version

## Compression Techniques

| Pattern | Before | After |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Redundant modifier | "completely eliminate" | "eliminate" |
| Wordy phrase | "due to the fact that" | "because" |
| Passive voice | "was decided by the team" | "the team decided" |
| Unnecessary "that" | "I think that we should" | "I think we should" |
| Weak opening | "There are many reasons why" | "Many reasons explain" |

## Example

**Original** (67 words):
> In the current business environment, it has become increasingly important for organizations to really focus on the customer experience. This is due to the fact that customers now have more choices than ever before, and they are not hesitant to switch to competitors if they feel that their needs are not being adequately met by the current provider.

**Compressed** (38 words, -43%):
> Customer experience is now critical. With more choices than ever, customers quickly switch to competitors when their needs aren't met. Organizations that ignore this reality lose market share to those who prioritize it.

## What NOT to Cut

- ✅ Keep specific data/numbers
- ✅ Keep names and proper nouns
- ✅ Keep unique insights or perspectives
- ✅ Keep emotional resonance if intentional
