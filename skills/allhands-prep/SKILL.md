# All-Hands Preparation

Helps prepare structured, energizing all-hands meetings following Beam's established guidelines and format.

## Workflow

### 1. Understand the Context

Ask the user:
- **Theme**: What's the one clear theme for this all-hands? (e.g., "Customer Obsession", "Execution Standards", "Strategic Shift")
- **Date**: When is the all-hands scheduled?
- **Goal**: What behavioral shift or outcome do you want from the team?
- **Previous all-hands**: Any context from recent all-hands that this projects on or contrasts with?

### 2. Structure the Meeting

Follow the standard structure (25-35 minutes max):

| Section | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Opening | 2 mins | Set energy and theme |
| Team Updates | 5-7 mins | Quick wins and what's coming |
| Main Topic | 10-15 mins | Core message of this all-hands |
| The Ask | 3 mins | What we need from everyone |
| Close | 2 mins | Land the message, create momentum |

### 3. Draft Content

For each section, help draft:
- **Opening**: Quick energy check, connect to mission/values, introduce theme
- **Team Updates**: Remind leads of format (60-90 sec: one shipped, one coming, one number)
- **Main Topic**: Frame the why, make it concrete with examples, show team effort, connect to values
- **The Ask**: Specific asks for each team (Engineering, Solutions, Product, GTM)
- **Close**: One memorable line that lands the message

### 4. Create Prep Checklist

Generate checklist with:
- **1 week before**: Confirm theme, identify speakers, start deck
- **3 days before**: Slides complete, notify department leads, prep featured speakers
- **1 day before**: Run-through with speakers, final slide review, calendar reminder
- **Day of**: Tech check 10 mins before, start on time

### 5. Slide Outline

Suggest 8-10 slides max:
1. Title (Theme + Beam logo)
2. Mission/values connection
3. Framework or model (if applicable)
4. Team effort visual
5. Customer/example slides (1 per example, keep tight)
6. The Ask (what we need from each team)
7. Close (one memorable line)

## Key Principles

**Respect time**: 25-35 minutes max. If it can't be said in 35 minutes, it's too much.

**One message**: People remember one thing. Make sure you know what that one thing is.

**Show, don't tell**: Real examples > abstract concepts. Name names. Share specifics.

**Energy is contagious**: The tone you bring is the tone the team will carry out.

**Forward motion only**: All-hands should leave people energized to act, not drained.

## Tone Guidelines

- **Energy level: 8/10** - Fired up, but not manic. Confident, not desperate.
- **Pace: Brisk** - Don't linger. Forward motion in delivery mirrors forward motion in message.
- **Be specific** - No vague "let's do our best" - actual examples, actual names, actual expectations.

## What to Avoid

❌ "We're going to try to..." → Say "We're doing..."
❌ "I hope everyone can..." → Say "Here's what I need from you..."
❌ "Does this sound good?" → Say "This is the direction. Questions?"
❌ Long explanations of problems → Brief context, then focus on solutions
